Why Dev Bootcamp Now Fox-Rosen?

This seems like the appropriate opportunity for an ordered list.

  1. I've been working as a freelance musician for 10 years, doing A LOT of music related things to make a living. Many bands, many shows, much teaching, recording, events, etc etc etc. And it's getting harder to actually make a living doing what I love.
  2. I never would have thought I would become a developer in the past. But I was evaluating where to go and what to do next for employment.
    Criteria For Evaluating What I Will Do Next:
    1. creative work
    2. with creative people
    3. that will not be downsized in the foreseeable future
  3. And waddya know? That sounds like dev work. So I been studying, and learning. My girlfriend is an illustrator and developer, and more or less able to balance both. And ideally that's what I want. I have no delusions about being the best, or making a bazillion dollar with my
    genius startup idea.
    I just want security, in a creative field, that won't view my last 10 years of artsy fartsy ass kicking as a weakness.
